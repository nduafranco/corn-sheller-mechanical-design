# Manual Corn Sheller: Mechanical Design & Drafting

## Project Overview
This repository showcases the mechanical design and technical drafting of a manual rotary maize sheller. The system is engineered to provide a high-efficiency solution for kernel removal in small-scale agricultural settings, focusing on ease of assembly and rotational smoothness.

## Key Engineering Features
* **Rotational Efficiency:** Integrated a standard $\varnothing 60$ mm ball bearing assembly to minimize friction during manual operation.
* **Mechanical Leverage:** Optimized the hand-crank handle with $R10$ mm fillet radii to provide ergonomic leverage while reducing stress concentrations at the bends.
* **Precision Shaft Interface:** Designed a $\varnothing 10$ mm drive shaft to securely transmit torque to the shelling drum.
* **Compact Footprint:** Total assembly length of $174$ mm ensures the device is portable and easily mountable.

## Technical Specifications
* **Shelling Drum:** Features a specialized "Corn Sheller Part" with radial teeth designed for maximum throughput.
* **Bearing Housing:** Features a $\varnothing 60$ mm Outer Diameter with internal features at $\varnothing 36$ mm and $\varnothing 32$ mm to ensure a precision fit.
* **Drafting Standard:** Produced detailed orthographic views with standardized dimensioning and a comprehensive Parts List.
